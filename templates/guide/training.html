Case Management

What is a Case?

A structured record of a real business problem — what happened, the context, and how it was resolved. Cases build the team's collective knowledge over time.

Creating a case

Training → Cases → New Case. Fill in the title, problem description, context (what triggered it), and resolution. Tag it to one or more departments.

Convert from a lead

On a lead's detail page, write notes in the Case Notes card. Once AI-cleaned, click "Convert to Case" — the form pre-fills automatically from your notes.

Customer link

Cases can be optionally linked to a customer record. Useful when searching for past issues with a specific account.

Quiz System

Taking a quiz

Training → Quizzes. You'll see quiz sets relevant to your department. Click one and answer each question in your own words — no need to match exact phrasing.

AI-judged answers

The AI compares your answer against the correct answer for meaning, not exact text. If you're wrong, it explains why and shows what was expected.

Pass threshold

Score 70% or above to pass. Results show Pass or Fail alongside your score and explanations.

Best score tracked

Your highest score per quiz set is recorded. Retaking and scoring higher updates your best — it doesn't reset it.

Knowledge Q&A

What it does

Ask a question in plain language and get an answer sourced from uploaded company documents — catalogues, SOPs, policies, past case resolutions, steel technical references, and more.

Not a chatbot

This is a single question-and-answer lookup, not a conversation. Each question is independent — there is no chat history or follow-up context carried between questions.

Source documents

Answers come from documents uploaded and processed by an admin. If a topic isn't covered, the AI will say so rather than guess.

Department filtering

Documents are tagged by department. You'll only receive answers from documents relevant to your team's work.

Knowledge Base → Ask a Question →

What's in the Knowledge Base

Supported document types

PDF files, Word documents (.docx), and plain text. Excel files are not directly supported — convert relevant tables to text before uploading.

What gets indexed

Product catalogues, pricing guidelines, company SOPs, steel technical references, competitor notes, past case resolutions, and FAQ documents.

Processing time

After upload, a document needs to be "processed" before it's searchable. This extracts text and builds the search index — an admin triggers this step.

Admin — Managing Training Content

Documents
Uploading

Training → Knowledge Base → Upload Document. Choose a file or paste text directly. Tag with departments and keywords.

Processing

Open the document detail and click Process. This indexes the document for Q&A. Required before it becomes searchable.

Quiz Sets & Questions
Creating a quiz set

Training → Quizzes → New Quiz Set. Give it a title, description, and department tags. Then add questions to it.

Writing questions

Questions need a clear correct answer — this is what the AI judges user responses against. Write it as a complete, readable answer, not a keyword list.

Cases
Adding cases

Admins and Team Leads can create, edit, and delete cases. All users can view them. Cases are also a source for Q&A answers.

Department tags

Tag each case to the relevant departments. This controls which team members see it in filtered views and Q&A results.

## 1. Overview

Module 3 — Training + Case Solver — is the institutional knowledge system for Ferrite Steel. It exists to solve two common problems:

1. **Repeated mistakes** — when a problem gets solved once, no one writes it down. The next person faces the same situation from scratch.
2. **Slow onboarding** — new staff have no structured way to learn how the business works.

Module 3 has three parts:

| Part | What it does | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **3A — Cases** | Record and browse past problem-resolution cases | Live |
| **3B — Quizzes** | Structured quizzes with AI-judged answers | Live |
| **3C — Knowledge Q&A** | Ask questions; AI answers from uploaded documents | Coming soon |

## 3. Sub-module 3A — Cases

### 3.1 What is a Case?

A **Case** is a structured record of a real situation that happened at Ferrite Steel — a difficult customer, an unusual order, a pricing dispute, a delivery problem — together with how it was resolved. Cases are written so that any staff member can read them and know what to do if the same situation comes up again.

### 3.2 Browsing Cases

Go to **Training → Cases**. The case list shows all cases relevant to your department(s).

- **Search** by keyword to find cases by topic
- **Filter by department** to see cases from a specific team
- Click any case to read the full record

### 3.3 Reading a Case

A case record contains:

| Field | What it means |
|---|---|
| Title | Short name for the situation |
| Problem Description | What happened — the specific issue |
| Context | What triggered it (customer, order, product, etc.) |
| Resolution | Exactly what was done to resolve it |
| Departments | Which teams this case is relevant to |
| Customer | The customer involved (if applicable) |
| Created by | Who wrote it |

### 3.4 Creating a Case Manually

Only **team leads** and **admins** can create, edit, or delete cases.

1. Go to **Training → Cases**
2. Click **+ New Case**
3. Fill in all fields:
   - **Title** — brief, searchable name (e.g. "Customer disputed rate after quotation sent")
   - **Problem Description** — describe the problem clearly
   - **Context** — what triggered the situation
   - **Resolution** — what was done step by step
   - **Departments** — tick all teams this is relevant to
   - **Customer** — link to the customer record if applicable
4. Click **Save**

### 3.5 Creating a Case from a Lead

When you deal with an unusual situation on a lead, you can convert your lead notes directly into a case:

1. Open the **Lead Detail** page
2. In the **Case Notes** card, write your notes in the Raw Notes box (you can use voice dictation — see Module 2 manual, Section 9)
3. Click **✦ Clean up with AI** to get a polished version
4. Click **→ Convert to Case**
5. The Case creation form opens pre-filled with your notes
6. Review, adjust, and click **Save**

> This is the recommended way to create cases — start from a real lead, not from scratch.

### 3.6 Editing or Deleting a Case

From the Case Detail page:
- Click **Edit** to update any field
- Click **Delete** and confirm — this is permanent

Only team leads and admins can edit or delete cases.

## 4. Sub-module 3B — Quizzes

### 4.1 What is a Quiz?

A **Quiz** is a set of questions created by an admin to test knowledge. Questions may cover product knowledge, customer handling, pricing rules, company policies, or anything relevant to the job. Your answers are judged by AI — not a rigid keyword match — so natural answers are accepted as long as they are correct.

### 4.2 Browsing Quizzes

Go to **Training → Quizzes**. You see quiz sets relevant to your department(s). Each card shows:
- The quiz title and description
- Number of questions
- Your best score (if you've taken it before)
- Whether you have passed it

### 4.3 Taking a Quiz

1. Click a quiz set to open its detail page
2. Click **Start Quiz** (or **Retake** if you've done it before)
3. Answer each question in your own words — there is no multiple choice
4. Click **Submit** when done

The AI judges each answer against the correct answer. For each question you got wrong, it explains the correct answer so you can learn.

**Passing score:** 70% or higher.

> You can retake a quiz as many times as you like. Your **best** score is always shown on the quiz list.

### 4.4 Viewing Results

After submitting, you see your results immediately:
- Your score (e.g. 8/10 — 80%)
- Whether you passed
- For each incorrect answer: the question, what the correct answer is, and why

Your best attempt is saved and shown on the quiz list card.

### 4.5 Quiz Availability

Quizzes are assigned to departments. You only see quiz sets that are relevant to your team — a CS Team member will not see Market Team quizzes and vice versa.

## 6. Who Sees What

### Viewing cases

All staff (members, leads, admins) can browse and read cases. There are no restrictions on reading.

### Creating, editing, deleting cases

Only **team leads** and **admins** can create, edit, or delete cases.

### Quizzes

All staff can take quizzes. Only admins can create or manage quiz sets and questions (via the admin panel).

### Department filtering

Both cases and quizzes are tagged with departments. You only see records tagged for your team. Admins see all records regardless of department.
